Valen vs Fitbod

Both hand you a plan instead of a blank screen. The difference is what it costs, and where your data lives.

Written to be fair. Pricing verified July 2026.

The short version

Fitbod is the most polished automatic workout generator in the category — a mature algorithm, a 1,600-exercise library, HD video for every movement, and eleven years of refinement behind it. If you want a proven, hands-off generator and the price doesn't bother you, it's an easy recommendation.

Valen is younger and smaller, and honest about it. It does two things Fitbod doesn't: it keeps your training data on your phone rather than on a server, and its coach is a single honest voice that talks to you across the whole app instead of silently generating a session. If privacy and a coach that actually says something matter more to you than library size, Valen is the one to try.

Where Fitbod is genuinely ahead

We're not going to pretend otherwise. Fitbod's library is roughly eight times the size of ours, and every movement has an HD video demo — which matters a lot if you're new and don't know what an exercise looks like. Its algorithm has had eleven years and over 150 million logged workouts to mature. And it's on both platforms today, where Valen is Android-first with iOS still coming.

If your priority is the deepest, most proven automatic generator and you'll happily pay for it, Fitbod has earned that position.

Where Valen is different

Your data never leaves your phone. Fitbod, like every mainstream tracker, keeps your full history on its servers and syncs your body metrics from Apple Health. Valen stores your workouts, body weight and history only on your device — we have no copy and can't see them. That's not a setting; it's how the app is built.

The coach actually talks. Fitbod has no conversational coach — it generates a session and leaves you to it. Valen's coach is one consistent character across programme building, your post-workout debrief and general chat, and it won't flatter a bad week. Some people want a silent generator; if you want something that feels like a coach, that's the gap.

You can start free, and without an account. Fitbod's free tier is a 7-day trial that then stops working. Valen's tracking is free and needs no sign-up at all — you only make an account if you want the coach.

Choose Fitbod if…

You want the most mature hands-off generator, you value a huge library with video for every exercise, you're on iOS today, and the price isn't a barrier. It's the safe, polished pick.

Choose Valen if…

You want your training data to stay private on your device, you'd rather have a coach that speaks honestly than an algorithm that stays silent, and you want to start free without handing over an account. You'll trade library size for privacy and a real voice.
