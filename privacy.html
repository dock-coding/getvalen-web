Valen keeps your training data on your phone.

Your workouts, programmes, personal bests, body weight and profile are stored only on your device. We never upload them to a server, and we cannot see them.

Two things do leave your phone: your email address, if you create an account — and your message to the AI coach, together with a summary of your training, when you ask it a question. Nothing else, ever.

03 What stays on your device

The following is stored locally on your phone only. It is never uploaded to us. We have no copy of it, we cannot access it, and we cannot recover it for you:

  • Your name and training goal
  • Your height, body weight and body-weight history, date of birth, and biological sex
  • Your training location and available equipment
  • All workouts, sets, reps and weights
  • Programmes you build or follow
  • Personal bests, strength scores, badges and milestones
  • Water intake
  • App settings and theme preferences

But read section 4.3 as well. If — and only if — you use the AI coach, some of the above is summarised and sent to Anthropic so the coach has something to work with. Your height, your date of birth, your surname and your body weight as a number are never sent. Your age, your biological sex, your goal, and a summary of your recent training are. Section 4.3 lists it field by field.

A consequence worth understanding. Because we hold no copy, we cannot restore this data for you. If you uninstall Valen, change phone, or clear the app's data, your training history is gone — unless you have made a backup yourself. Valen now lets you export an encrypted backup file that you control; see section 3.1. We still never receive a copy, and we still cannot recover your data if you lose both your phone and your backup. That is the trade-off that comes with data that never leaves your phone without your say-so.

3.1   Backing up your data

Valen lets you export a backup file containing the data in section 3, so you can move to a new phone or guard against losing everything. You do this yourself, from within the app, and you decide where the file goes.

The backup is encrypted with a passphrase you choose. The file is scrambled on your phone before it is saved, using your passphrase. Without that passphrase the file is unreadable — it is just noise. Anyone who gets hold of the file, including us, cannot open it.

Two things follow from that, and both matter.

We cannot recover your backup, and we cannot reset the passphrase. We never receive the file and we never see your passphrase. If you forget it, the backup is gone for good — there is no "forgot password" link, because there is nothing on our side to reset. Choose a passphrase you will remember, and keep it somewhere safe.

Once you export it, the file is yours to look after. It might contain health-related information — including the coach's notes about any injuries or limitations you have told it about (see 4.3.1). It is encrypted, so it is protected even if it falls into the wrong hands, but where you store it — your own cloud drive, a computer, a USB stick — is your choice and outside our control. Store it somewhere you trust.

A backup file never comes to us and never goes to Anthropic or any other provider. It travels only where you send it. Restoring from a backup happens entirely on your phone: you open the file in Valen, enter your passphrase, and your data is decrypted back onto the device.

Some things are deliberately left out of the file. Anything Valen can work out again for itself is not included — your personal bests, for instance, are rebuilt from your workout history when you restore, rather than carried in the file. Nor is anything that belongs to one particular phone: a workout you had in progress, or internal housekeeping. And your sign-in details are never in a backup. Restoring a file onto a phone does not sign you in, and cannot.

Your consent to use the coach does not travel in a backup either. Permission to send health information to the coach is asked for on each phone, and is not carried in the file. Restore your data onto a new device and Valen will ask you again before the coach will work.

That is deliberate. Consent to use health data should be something you give knowingly, on the device in your hand — not something a file grants quietly on your behalf.

3.2   The safety copy kept when you restore

Restoring a backup replaces everything currently on your phone. So that a mistake can be undone, Valen first takes a safety copy of the data already on the device, and offers you an Undo Last Restore button.

  • The safety copy is held on your phone only. It is never sent to us or anyone else.
  • Unlike a backup file, it is not encrypted with a passphrase. It never leaves the device, and it exists only so the app can put your own data back where it was.
  • It is kept for up to 7 days, then deleted automatically.
  • It is also deleted the moment you use Undo Last Restore, and when you erase your local data or delete your account.

3.3   Sign-in diagnostics

This applies during the Valen beta only, and will be removed before general release — along with this section of the policy.

We are chasing a fault where a sign-in code occasionally fails to send. To find it, Valen keeps a short technical log of sign-in attempts on your phone. Each entry records the time, whether the attempt worked, what kind of error occurred if it did not, and a masked version of your email address — the first letter and the domain only, like i***@example.com.

  • The log stays on your device and is never sent to us automatically. It reaches us only if you choose to copy it and send it while reporting a problem.
  • Only the 20 most recent attempts are kept, and anything older than 30 days is deleted automatically.
  • It is deleted when you delete your account, and when you erase your local data.
  • It is not cleared when you simply sign out — otherwise the very fault you might be reporting would be erased before we could see it.

4.3.1   What you tell the coach, and what it remembers

This is the part people do not expect, so we want to be blunt about it.

Anything you say to the coach is sent to Anthropic. There is no filter. If you mention an injury, pain, an illness, a medication, or anything else about your health, that goes too — because a coach can only work around your bad shoulder if you tell it about your bad shoulder. Valen will sometimes ask you about injuries when it builds you a programme. You do not have to answer.

The coach also keeps notes. When it learns something durable about you — an injury, a limitation, a preference, what you are actually training for — it writes itself a short note so it does not have to ask you twice. Those notes are stored on your phone, and they are sent to Anthropic again every time you talk to the coach, not just on the day you mentioned it. A note that your knee is bad will keep travelling with your messages for as long as it is useful.

The notes live on your device. Erasing your local data (section 9) deletes them.

If you never use the coach, none of this happens. Nothing in section 4.3 applies to you unless you sign in, consent, and send a message. Everything else in Valen works without an account.

05 Health data and your consent

Some of what Valen handles — your body weight, your training history, and anything you tell the coach about injuries or illness — counts as health data under UK and EU data protection law, which gives it stronger protection than ordinary personal data.

While that information stays on your phone, we are not processing it at all, and no special rules are engaged. The same is true of a backup file you export (section 3.1): it is encrypted with your passphrase, it never reaches us, and we do not process what is inside it.

The moment it matters is when you use the coach, because that is when health-related information is transmitted to Anthropic. For that we rely on your explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a) UK GDPR). You give that consent before you can use the coach for the first time, and we tell you plainly at that point what will be sent and to whom.

We have cut what we can. Your body weight now travels only as a direction of travel, never as a number; your height and surname do not travel at all. But we will not pretend the coach can work without health information, and we would rather say so than bury it. Injuries and pain are the clearest example: if you want the coach to work around them, it has to know about them, and that means Anthropic sees them. That is the trade, stated plainly, and it is why we ask rather than assume.

Consent is genuinely optional. Valen's workout tracking, programmes, personal bests, milestones and everything else in section 3 work fully without an account and without consent. Declining costs you the coach and nothing else.

You can withdraw consent at any time — by signing out, or by deleting your account. Withdrawing it does not affect anything that has already happened, and it does not touch your training data. Your workouts stay on your device either way.

07 What we do not do

These are choices, not accidents:

  • No analytics. Valen contains no analytics SDK. We do not know how many workouts you log, which screens you visit, or how often you open the app.
  • No crash reporting. We collect no crash or telemetry data. During the beta the app keeps a small sign-in log on your phone (section 3.3), but it stays there — it is never sent to us unless you choose to send it.
  • No advertising. No ad networks, no ad identifiers, no ad tracking.
  • No third-party tracking of any kind. There are no tracking SDKs in the app.

  • No storage of your coach conversations. We keep counts, not content.
  • No AI training on your data. Neither we nor Anthropic use what you send to train models.

09 Deleting your account and your data

You have two separate controls and they do different things. That distinction is deliberate.

Delete your account

In the app: Profile → Settings → Delete Account.

This permanently deletes your account, your email address, and your usage counts from our systems. It cannot be undone. You will be signed out and Valen Coach will be locked.

It also clears the two things Valen keeps on your phone that are tied to having an account: the sign-in diagnostics log (section 3.3), and any safety copy left over from a restore (section 3.2).

Your workouts stay on your device. Deleting your account does not touch your training history. If you no longer want the AI coach, you can delete your account and carry on using Valen exactly as before, with everything you have logged intact.

Erase your local data

In the app: Profile → Settings → Erase All Data on This Device.

This wipes everything in section 3 — workouts, programmes, personal bests, profile, the lot. It also clears the safety copy from any restore (3.2) and the sign-in diagnostics log (3.3), so nothing Valen holds on the device is left behind. It is permanent, and we cannot recover it, because we never had it. Uninstalling the app has the same effect.

It does not touch any backup file you have already exported. Those are stored wherever you put them, and only you can delete them.

If you have already uninstalled Valen

You can still have your account deleted. Email privacy@getvalen.app from the address you signed up with, asking us to delete your account. We will delete it and confirm within one month, and usually a great deal sooner.

10 Your rights

Under UK and EU data protection law you have the right to access the personal data we hold about you, correct it if it is wrong, delete it (section 9), restrict or object to how we use it, receive it in a machine-readable format (portability), and withdraw consent where we rely on it (section 5).

In practice, the data we hold about you is small: your email address, an account identifier, sign-in timestamps, and monthly usage counts. Everything else is on your phone, where it is already yours.

That includes the coach's notes about you (4.3.1). They are on your device, not ours. Valen does not yet give you a screen to read them back — we are building one, because you should be able to see what your coach thinks it knows. In the meantime, erasing your local data (section 9) deletes them, and you can email us and ask.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@getvalen.app. We will respond within one month.

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your data, you can complain to the Information Commissioner's Office, the UK's data protection regulator.

11 Children

Valen is for adults. You must be 18 or over to use it. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18. If you believe someone under 18 has created an account, email privacy@getvalen.app and we will delete it.

Two reasons, and neither is a formality. The coach gives strength-training advice, which is not something we are willing to put in front of a child. And the coach only works with your explicit consent to handle health information — a decision an adult should be making for themselves.

12 Security

Sign-in uses a one-time code sent to your email, so there is no password to steal. All communication between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit. Coaching requests are authenticated, so an account can only ever see its own usage.

We keep as little as possible, which is itself a security measure: data we do not hold cannot be breached.

No system is perfectly secure, and we will not pretend otherwise. If a breach ever affects your personal data, we will notify you and the ICO as the law requires.

When paid subscriptions launch, payments will be handled by Google Play and by our subscription provider. We will never see or store your card details. This policy will be updated before that happens.
